# StoryForge Training Data Sources

This document records the literary sources used to extract the narrative patterns, archetypes, and conversational data that power the StoryForge validation, benchmarking, and model adapters.

## Epic Fantasy & Adventure
These sources were used to train StoryForge's core structural generation, particularly for long-form narrative pacing, vast worldbuilding, and multi-layered quest chains.
* **Literary Sources Used:** *The Lord of the Rings*, *The Hobbit*
* **Author:** J.R.R. Tolkien
* **Purpose of Usage:** Worldbuilding, Quest Structures, Mythological Archetypes, Narrative Pacing

## Dark Fantasy
Used to train morally ambiguous character motivations, tense dialogue exchanges, and dynamic faction relationships.
* **Literary Sources Used:** *The Last Wish (The Witcher Series)*
* **Author:** Andrzej Sapkowski
* **Purpose of Usage:** Conflict Structures, Character Archetypes, Dialogue Patterns

## Mystery & Horror
Used to refine the model's ability to maintain suspense, withhold information gracefully, and generate compelling villain archetypes.
* **Literary Sources Used:** *Dracula*
* **Author:** Bram Stoker
* **Purpose of Usage:** Suspense Building, Pacing, Villain Archetypes

## Gothic Sci-Fi & Character Drama
Leveraged to train emotional resonance, internal character conflict, and ethical dilemmas within narrative events.
* **Literary Sources Used:** *Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus*
* **Author:** Mary Shelley
* **Purpose of Usage:** Character Drama, Internal Conflict, Emotional Resonance

## Political Intrigue
Used exclusively to extract rulesets, logic trees, and vocabulary for the Faction Builder and World State modules.
* **Literary Sources Used:** *The Prince*
* **Author:** Niccolò Machiavelli
* **Purpose of Usage:** Political Dynamics, Faction Goal Structures, Power Struggles

## Mythology
Leveraged for heroic templates, archaic naming conventions, and establishing grand narrative stakes.
* **Literary Sources Used:** *Beowulf: An Anglo-Saxon Epic Poem*
* **Author:** Anonymous (Classic)
* **Purpose of Usage:** Heroic Archetypes, High-Stakes Conflict, Thematic Profiles
